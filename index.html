A message from the candidates

Moving Forward— We believe that students deserve a real voice at Georgetown: an advocate for issues that actually affect us. GUSA spends too much time worrying about its own bureaucratic concerns rather than bringing students together to fully address issues like accessibility, affordability, and student health and wellness. Made up of an often-insular Executive and the same group of Senators each year, GUSA has failed to empower every student to have an active role in shaping the Georgetown community. We want to combine our energy and institutional knowledge with perspectives from every corner of campus in order to truly empower all Georgetown students. That’s why we’re running for student body president and vice-president–because GUSA should be the student association. We plan to elevate every voice as an important tool in these conversations. It’s time to move GUSA forward–to truly make it our student association–so we can all move forward together.

Meet The Team

Get to know the candidates and the people who stand behind them

Josh Sirois is a sophomore in the School of Foreign Service majoring in Global Business. He hails from a small town in rural Missouri where he spent all of his life before moving to DC. Ever since he arrived on campus, he has been actively pursuing his interests inside and (more frequently) outside of the classroom. When he should be studying, he can more often be found singing with Georgetown Superfood, working in the Office of Undergraduate Admissions, or gossiping with Ms. Veda at Crop Chop. He still can’t believe that he found a place here on the Hilltop where he is so free to be himself, and he is forever grateful for the opportunity to be a Hoya.

Josh has focused the majority of his time and attention toward making our campus a better place in the GUSA Senate and beyond, and he would be absolutely honored to have the opportunity to continue making progress and moving forward together with Casey and their team.

Casey Doherty is a sophomore in the College from a small town in upstate New York studying Government and American Studies. Casey feels so fortunate to have found a home on the Hilltop from her first moments on campus thanks to the GOLD pre-orientation program. In her two years on campus, she has given back to the organizations and students that mean so much to her, now serving as Coordinator of GOLD and continuing her involvement with GUSA's Federal and D.C. Relations Committee and the College Academic Council. Her activities on campus share a common theme: giving back to the student body. That's what she hopes to do as your next GUSA Vice-President.

Casey is so grateful for the opportunities Georgetown has afforded her and the valuable relationships she has formed with her peers. She can't think of a better way to serve the Georgetown community than with Josh in the GUSA Executive, implementing the policy points that really can make Georgetown a better place and help as all move forward together.
